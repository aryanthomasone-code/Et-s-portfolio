NOOR AL BAHAR

STRUCTURAL LIGHTING SYSTEM

YEAR // 2024

TYPE // LIGHTING DESIGN

PROJECT SUMMARY

Noor Al Bahar (Light of the Sea) is a team project of four, originally developed for a competition, that transforms discarded fishing nets into luminous objects rooted in Emirati heritage. By combining resin-stabilized nets with light inspired by pearl diving, it reimagines marine waste as functional, culturally expressive design.

01 / OVERVIEW

Noor Al Bahar (Light of the Sea) is a team project of four, originally developed for a competition, that transforms discarded fishing nets into luminous objects rooted in Emirati heritage. By combining resin-stabilized nets with light inspired by pearl diving, it reimagines marine waste as functional, culturally expressive design.

02 / SYSTEM

Material Transformation
The core of the project lies in repurposing abandoned fishing nets collected from UAE coastlines.
Discarded synthetic nets are restructured and preserved using resin
The material is stabilized while retaining its organic, woven texture
Each piece carries the irregularity and identity of its previous use
This process transforms harmful waste into a durable, design-ready material.

Light as Cultural Symbol
Lighting elements are integrated within the net structure to reference the region’s pearl diving heritage.
Light sources are embedded to resemble pearls within the net
Illumination diffuses through the woven material
Creates a soft, ambient glow inspired by oceanic patterns
The result is both functional and symbolic, connecting past traditions with present design.

Form & Expression
The fixture draws from natural marine geometries:
Organic, flowing compositions
Irregular structures reflecting ocean movement
Each piece is inherently unique
This ensures the product feels crafted rather than manufactured, reinforcing its cultural narrative.

03 / DESIGN INTENT

Noor Al Bahar is an exercise in cultural translation and material redemption. We wanted to challenge the perception of marine debris by turning a symbol of ecological harm—the discarded fishing net—into an object of beauty and utility. Drawing from the UAE's history of pearl diving, the design uses light to evoke the luminescence of pearls trapped within organic, oceanic forms, linking past traditions with present environmental consciousness.

OVERALL THOUGHTS

Although not formally entered into competition, Noor Al Bahar remains an ongoing project with strong potential for real-world production. It demonstrates how culturally rooted design can address environmental challenges while creating products that are both meaningful and market-ready.
