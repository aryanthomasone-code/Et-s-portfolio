OVERVIEW

A showcase of creative direction and experimental aesthetics. Exploring powerful thematic narratives through light, spatial context, and conceptual composition.

02 / CARPET DESIGNS

All patterns are tileable — scroll vertically to explore variants

This was the selected carpet design, developed in 2025 with a few refinements along the way. The goal was to create a more modern prayer carpet, drawing inspiration from Islamic architecture and translating it into a contemporary pattern.After multiple iterations, this version was chosen. It is fully tileable, with size adjustments made per the manufacturers’ request. The prayer section, inspired by South Asian Islamic architecture, was added later based on their feedback. Many color variations were explored, but this one ultimately made it through the gauntlet.

These carpets are iterations of the same pattern. While this version isn’t tileable, it was created as a test piece after a joke from the professor and manufacturers about making an “emo” carpet. Given my natural inclination toward punk and grunge aesthetics, this direction came quite easily. It wasn’t selected, but it served as a fun experiment, created through a mix of kitbashing and drawn-over color work. This was developed later on, after the competition switched manufacturers.

This was one of the earlier carpet patterns I developed, it progressed quite far but didn’t make the final selection. It drew heavy inspiration from The Starry Night and went through multiple variations and colorways, with this version being the strongest, though it ultimately wasn’t chosen.

To say I was on a kick with the dark carpet aesthetic would be an understatement. This piece was part of a collection for my hypothetical brand, where I pushed a more regal, “filthy rich” feel. It is fully tileable, featuring a floral centerpiece and corner elements that form an abstract rider on a horse when repeated. It’s one of my more interesting tileable patterns, though it was more of a personal exploration, especially since the inclusion of a figure made it less suitable as a prayer carpet.

This piece was created by kitbashing rectangles and façade elements as part of a collection for my hypothetical brand, which was very well received. It is fully tileable and explores abstraction more than the other carpet designs. While the intended architectural influence is still present, it also stands on its own as a unique composition, balancing abstraction with a sense of controlled chaos, which was the direction I aimed for.

This piece was created through a mix of Vizcom kitbashing, multiple iterations, and cleanups, resulting in a fully tileable floral pattern. It was developed as part of a hypothetical collection for my brand in class, but was well received by both the manufacturers and my professor, so I chose to keep it. I’m still unsure if it will secure another spot in the competition, but it presents an interesting take on the floral aesthetic leaning into a more chaotic, regal feel that aligns closely with my brand’s direction.

03 / DATA VISUALIZATION

This infographic was created for a class project where my team researched scam calls. I designed it, using an aggressive color palette to reflect the urgency and severity of the issue. The aim was to present the information clearly while still conveying the impact, resulting in a clean yet visually striking composition.
