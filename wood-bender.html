WOOD BENDER

ROBOTIC FABRICATION SYSTEM

YEAR // 2024

TYPE // ENGINEERING

PROJECT SUMMARY

Wood Bending is a robotic fabrication system that explores how minimal material can be transformed into complex, lightweight structures through controlled deformation and bonding. Using a KUKA 6-axis robotic arm, wooden strips are incrementally bent into multi-axis forms and fixed in place with UV-curable resin, creating intricate, nest-like geometries. By integrating sensing, modular design, and a combined bending–bonding workflow, the project enables efficient, scalable, and expressive construction beyond traditional wood fabrication methods.

01 / OVERVIEW

Wood Bending investigates how minimal material can be transformed into strong, complex structures using robotic assistance. Leveraging a KUKA 6-axis robotic arm, the project focuses on bending wooden elements into multi-axis forms while bonding them in place using UV-curable resin. The result is a scalable system capable of producing intricate, nest-like structures with high material efficiency. This was done as a team between me and Isra Tirmzi

02 / SYSTEM

Robotic Bending Mechanism
A roller-based end effector incrementally bends wooden strips into controlled, multi-axis forms through repeated passes. It adapts to different lengths and materials, drawing from bamboo construction principles for structural integrity.

Resin Bonding System
UV-curable resin is applied during bending to fix elements in place through extrusion, brief pre-curing, and full curing. Controlled pre-curing resolved early adhesion issues, improving strength and stability.

Sensor-Guided Fabrication
Sensors detect pre-marked points on wooden planks, allowing precise pausing and resin application for consistent, repeatable results without complex programming.

Modular & Scalable Design
The system is modular, scalable, flexible and easy to assemble, repair, and transport, while supporting varied configurations and architectural-scale applications.

03 / DESIGN INTENT

The project explores the intersection of robotic fabrication and material behavior, aiming to create structures that are both efficient and expressive. By combining bending and bonding into a single workflow, the system reduces material waste while enabling organic, free-form geometries that would be difficult to achieve through traditional methods. It was very intensive finding methods of bending wood due to existing ones such as steam bending and zip bending but also taking inspirations from metal bending and sheet metal buildings. We took aspects of all forms and merged it into something of our which would be used in architectural scale to build bird’s nest type structures.This could also be used for essentially most wooden architectural related projects

OVERALL THOUGHTS

Wood Bending demonstrates how robotic systems can extend the capabilities of natural materials. Though we went a lot of different routes many of which didn’t work we still made it through with a better technique and that’s what we learned. Working with intention. Through a combination of controlled deformation, adaptive bonding, and scalable design, the project proposes a new approach to lightweight structural fabrication.
